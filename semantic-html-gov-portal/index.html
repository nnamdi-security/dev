Welcome to Greenfield City – Where Nature Meets Opportunity!

It is with great joy and pride that we welcome you to Greenfield City — a modern, eco-friendly community designed for comfortable living, innovation, and sustainable growth.

Nestled within the peaceful and culturally rich environment of Greenfield City Council, Greenfield City is built on the values of unity, security, progress, and environmental responsibility. Here, green landscapes blend with smart infrastructure, creating a city where families thrive, businesses grow, and dreams become reality.

Whether you are a resident, investor, entrepreneur, or visitor, Greenfield City offers:
  • Clean and sustainable surroundings
  • Well-planned residential neighborhoods
  • Modern business and commercial districts
  • Quality educational and skill development opportunities
  • Vibrant markets and recreation centers
  • Safe and secure environment
We believe in building not just structures, but a strong and inclusive community where everyone has the opportunity to succeed.

Public Service Directory

Electricity and Energy Department

  • Greenfield City Council Electricity Regulatory Commission (EERC)
    • Show service description

    Regulates electricity generation, distribution, and supply within Greenfield City Council. It ensures fair pricing, licensing of operators, consumer protection, and improved power service delivery.

  • MainPower Electricity Distribution Limited
  • Rural Electrification Agency (REA) – State Projects
  • Fault Response & Maintenance Services
    • Show service details

    Handles complaints about power outages, damaged transformers, fallen poles, and electrical faults. Technical teams are deployed to repair and restore electricity supply quickly.

Water Supply Service — Greenfield City Council Water Corporation

  • Water Treatment & Purification
    • Show service description

    The Corporation treats raw water from sources (like rivers and boreholes) so it is safe and clean for drinking and domestic use. This involves filtering, disinfecting, and removing harmful substances.

  • Water Distribution
  • Meter Installation & Billing Services
  • Pipeline Maintenance & Repair
    • Show service desciprion

    This service involves fixing broken pipes, leaks, and damaged valves to ensure continuous water supply and prevent waste.

Education Service — The Greenfield City Council Ministry of Education

  • School Administration & Management
    • Show service description

    This includes overseeing the running of public primary and secondary schools, appointing staff, managing infrastructure, and ensuring that schools follow the state curriculum.

  • Teacher Recruitment & Training
    • Show service description

    The Ministry handles hiring qualified teachers and offering professional development programs, workshops, and training to improve teaching skills and classroom performance.

  • Curriculum Implementation & Assessment
    • Show service description

    Ensures that approved curricula are taught in schools and that student assessments, exams, and evaluations are administered fairly and effectively.

  • Student Support & Welfare Programs

Health Service — The Greenfield City Council Ministry of Health

  • Primary Healthcare Services
    • Show service description

    Provides basic medical care through primary health centres across the state, including treatment of common illnesses, maternal care, and child health services.

  • Immunization & Disease Prevention Programs
    • Show service description

    Organizes vaccination campaigns and public health programs to prevent diseases such as measles, polio, and malaria.

  • Hospital Management & Supervision
    • Show service descriptions

    Oversees state-owned hospitals and health facilities to ensure proper staffing, equipment availability, and quality healthcare delivery.

  • Health Education & Public Awareness
    • Show service description

    Conducts public sensitization campaigns on hygiene, sanitation, nutrition, and disease prevention to promote healthy living.

New Bridge Construction To Begin Next Month

February 1, 2026

The department of transportation has finalized the plans for the downtown bridge expansion at Abapka Junction. This project aims to reduce traffice conjestion at the intersection by 30% over the next two years

Environment

City Parks to Go 100% Solar-Powered

February 15, 2026

A new initiative was launched today to install high-efficiency solar panels across all city-manged parks and community centers.

"Our goal is to make this city a leader in renewable energy, starting with the very spaces our children play in"
- Hon. Mazi Sunshine Chikadibie

This transistion is expected to save the municipality over $200,000 in energy costs annually
